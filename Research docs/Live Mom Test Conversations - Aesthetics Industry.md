# Live Mom Test Conversations — Aesthetics Industry

> **Purpose**: Record real conversations with clinic staff/practitioners where we ask about their actual problems (not pitch solutions). After each conversation, map findings against the Development Plan to identify gaps, confirmations, or misalignments.
> **Format**: Date | Venue | Role of Interviewee | Key Problems Mentioned | Plan Alignment / Gap Analysis

---

## Conversation 1: Nuskinovation, Sandton

**Date**: April 2026
**Venue**: Nuskinovation, Sandton
**Interviewee Role**: All-rounder (answers calls, messages, assists practitioner)
**Conversation Method**: In-person / informal

### Raw Notes from Conversation
- She experiences time-wasters on WhatsApp, but **does not consider it a nuisance**.
- **Real nuisance = no-shows**. She explicitly stated this needs to be automated.
- **Aftercare automation** would be "nice to have." She mentioned patients forget aftercare instructions.
- She seems to be the front-desk / receptionist + assistant hybrid.

### Mom Test Analysis
| Question Implied | Answer | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| "What is your biggest pain point?" | No-shows | **Confirmed hair-on-fire problem** |
| "How do you handle tyre-kickers?" | WhatsApp time-wasters exist but tolerable | Tyre-kickers are a problem, but not the burning one |
| "What happens after a treatment?" | Patients forget aftercare | Aftercare follow-up is a real, acknowledged gap |
| "What would you automate first if you could?" | No-shows, then aftercare | Clear priority ranking |

### Plan Alignment Check

| Problem Mentioned | Do We Solve It? | Plan Reference | Gap / Confirmation |
|---|---|---|---|
| No-shows | **YES** | Phase 2: 3-layer reminder system (24h/4h/1h) + Yoco deposits + waitlist backfill | **Confirmed**. This is our #3 hair-on-fire problem. |
| Aftercare follow-up (patients forget) | **PARTIALLY** | Phase 2: Aftercare Messaging System (T+0h, T+24h, T+72h, T+7d) | **GAP IDENTIFIED**: Interviewee implied she wants **daily step-by-step aftercare** for the early days (Day 1, Day 2, Day 3...). Our current plan uses hour-based triggers (0h, 24h, 72h) but does not explicitly describe a **daily sequence** with progressive instructions. We should consider making aftercare configurable as a daily sequence (Day 1, Day 2, Day 3...) per service, not just arbitrary hour offsets. |
| WhatsApp time-wasters | **YES** | Phase 1: RAG FAQ + intent detection filters unqualified leads | Confirmed lower priority than no-shows. |

### Action Items from This Conversation
1. **Aftercare granularity**: Consider revising the aftercare model to support "Day N" sequences (Day 1, Day 2, Day 3...) with progressive instructions, rather than just hour offsets. This maps better to how clinic staff think about post-treatment care.
2. **No-show emphasis**: Continue prioritizing no-show automation. This is validated by real clinic staff.
3. **Photo triage in aftercare**: Interviewee did not mention photos, but our plan includes T+24h "Reply PHOTO if you'd like us to review." This is aligned. However, we should also consider that after Day 3, if a patient reports an issue, the bot should proactively ask for a photo and route to the practitioner.
